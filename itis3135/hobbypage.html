While the exact process of how my brother built his PC eludes my memory, I yearned to emulate him. I convinced my parents to hand down his old parts when he upgraded, hoping to follow in his footsteps.

When Did You Start?

My journey commenced when my brother upgraded his PC at 17, and being four years younger, I was 13 at the time. Witnessing the joy and camaraderie he experienced with friends while gaming incited jealousy in me, prompting a desire to have my own PC. Although I didn't delve into learning the specifics of building PCs until my sophomore year in high school, it was a computer engineering class that introduced me to the process. Years later, armed with that experience, I ventured into buying new parts for my PC.

Why Did You Do It?

Primarily driven by envy and a longstanding desire kindled by observing my brother, I yearned to craft my own PC. The longing to learn the art of PC building, nurtured over years of observation, finally materialized when my boredom intersected with a culmination of accumulated knowledge from classes and online tutorials.
